Diet changes
By eating the right food, you can manage your symptoms well. A well-balanced diet will regulate your hormones and menstrual cycle.
Start the day with healthy food
Begin your day with a healthy breakfast. Include leafy greens, proteins, and healthy fats to maintain balanced blood sugar levels.
Whole foods
Whole foods help maintain blood sugar levels and are free from artificial sugars and preservatives.
Soy it up
Soy acts like estrogen, helping balance hormones, but avoid soy if you have a family history of estrogen-related cancers.
Anti-inflammatory
PCOS is associated with chronic low-level inflammation. Include anti-inflammatory foods such as leafy greens, olive oil, and tomatoes.
Balance it out!
Proper intake of carbs and protein is crucial for energy and hormone regulation. Include magnesium-rich foods like bananas and spinach.
Add some fibre
A diet rich in fiber helps with digestion and weight management. Include foods like avocados, pears, and broccoli.
Up your iron intake
Heavy periods may lead to iron deficiency. Include iron-rich foods like spinach, eggs, and broccoli.
Say no to caffeine
Coffee may cause more harm than good. Switch to herbal teas to help with insulin resistance and weight management.
Turmeric
This spice contains curcumin, which acts as an anti-inflammatory and helps reduce insulin resistance.
Zinc
Zinc helps improve fertility and can reduce excessive hair growth. Include zinc-rich foods like seafood, nuts, and beans.
Evening primrose oil
This oil helps regulate menstruation, reduces period pain, and improves cholesterol levels.
Tulsi to the rescue!
Holy basil helps regulate blood sugar and prevent weight gain.
Avoid Endocrine Disruptors
Endocrine disruptors, such as those in plastics, affect hormone balance. Avoid these to support reproductive health.
Try Acupuncture
Acupuncture, combined with exercise, can improve hormone levels and menstrual frequency.
Exercise
Regular, moderate exercise can help maintain a healthy weight and balance hormones.
Reduce stress
Proper sleep and stress management through yoga, walks, and self-care can help maintain hormonal balance.
Take necessary precautions
Always consult with a doctor before starting any alternative treatments. Be cautious of false claims and excessive supplement use.
Be observant about your surroundings
Avoid toxins in body care products, and consider making your own natural products using ingredients like vinegar and essential oils.
